01

Thirty years in service. The rooms keep changing. The work underneath them doesn't.

The restaurant. Your father saw a homeless man walking past the front window and said "ASU, Go get him, sit him down on a table. I'll send you some food." Your mum would lose it - customers walking past, homeless guy in front. Your dad didn't care. The man got fed before the paying customers did. That was your first model of how to be in the world. Service as the way the household ran, before anyone called it a value.

The gurdwara. Your tradition has a word for what your dad was doing. Seva. Selfless service. He'd tell you "make sure you clean everybody's shoes." You called it "the lowliest of tasks." You remember it as "this weird sense of satisfaction and a weird kind of humility pride. Really satisfying. Really cool." Singh, your middle name, means lion. Warrior saint. Fierce and humble in the same breath. You repeated warrior saint three times in a single breath: "Being Sikh, we're spiritual warriors by nature. Warrior saints. It's like a huge thing."

Engineering. Seven years at Multiplex, 6am to 6pm, six days a week. 99.05% top of class, the J.N. McNichol Prize. You experienced the years as misalignment - the whole reason you eventually left. What stayed is the brain you built inside those buildings: structural thinking, load-bearing diagnostic, the discipline of figuring out what holds weight and what doesn't. The training stayed. You stopped doing it in buildings.

The work now. When you left engineering in January 2025, the coaching you'd been doing on the side for a decade became the full-time work. The podcast kept compounding - 300+ episodes, nine years, never missed a week, 1.2-2.4M monthly views. 400+ people coached over the decade. Then astrology arrived. Inside three months you'd done 116 readings. Your teacher Debra Silverman came on your podcast and told the audience your graduation reading was the best in the ten years she'd been running her school. You woke up the next day and had sold thirty readings.

Restaurant. Gurdwara. Multiplex. The Council. The readings. Same act underneath. The rooms keep changing.
02 - Your positioning
The Spiritual Servant
You're the spiritual servant to the people you've called "people to whom legacy matters" - the ones who've built something real, for whom the pursuit of alignment is no longer optional, and who need someone who can hold the chart, the patterns, the work, and the life simultaneously.
03 - Why The Spiritual Servant

Eastern work. Western vocabulary. The translation keeps stopping short.

Spiritual Engineer. Inner Engineer. Spiritual Architect. Spiritual Activist. Spiritual Guide. Spiritual Sherpa. You've circled this for months. Each one lands halfway and stops.

Each name has been an attempt to translate Eastern work into a Western vocabulary that doesn't fully hold it. Engineer for the pragmatists. Activist for the bold ones. Guide for the open ones. Each one trying to make the work legible to a Western spiritual market by softening or reshaping it into something the market already recognises. The work resists the fit, because the work has Eastern roots and the market is Western. Each English word you've reached for has tried to bridge that gap and stopped short.

Servant is the closest English carrier for seva. Not a direct translation - seva names the act of selfless service, sevadar names the person doing it - but the word that holds the meaning when seva has to travel into one English word. Every other English option dilutes it.

The risk is real and it's also the point. "Servant" in English carries baggage - subservience, low status. In the Sikh tradition, seva is the highest act - the dissolution of ego. The word holds both meanings simultaneously, and you've held both meanings in your body since childhood. Lion and shoe-cleaner. Warrior and saint. Singh and seva.

This is the paradox your audience already feels in you. They sense the engineer's brain and the depth practitioner at the same time. They sense the credentials and the humility check at the same time. They sense the warrior and the one who returns the power. They can't name what they're sensing because nobody has named it for them. The Spiritual Servant names it.

Spiritual Engineer is the strong second. You came up with it yourself, and it works - it captures the rigor under the spiritual content and it's immediately legible. The risk with Engineer is that it leads with the tool, not the orientation. Engineer is the second chapter of your story. Seva is the first.

This isn't a marketing claim. It's the first English title that carries the weight of what you've been doing since the restaurant.

Seva as professional orientation

10 - Where this could go

Five paths that could grow from the work you're doing right now.

Not a roadmap. Not a commitment. A map of what's already starting to take shape.

1. A defined counsel practice for prolific individuals. The clients who've come in through podcast relationships are the prototype. Naming the work, defining the cadence options, capping the number at 10-15 to protect depth. Highest-margin, lowest-time-load path.

2. The Council growing from 5 to 20-30 members. Retention has improved since the price drop and the quarterly deep dives. If the masterclass library proves out, the next layer is scaling membership while protecting the cadence that makes it work.

3. Experiences as a third Inspired Evolution stream. The 11/11 Egypt retreat is testing this. Intensive depth in person, podcast guests as co-facilitators. If it works, you have a model for one or two retreats a year.

4. AstroSelf - the platform play. The domain is yours. The methodology can run at scale if the platform is right. Longer horizon, different business. Doesn't need to be solved now.

5. The methodology as licensable IP. The keyword reconciliation, aetiology-over-symptomology, the Power Return Protocol, seva-grounded facilitation. Teachable. At some point, training other practitioners to run versions of the work becomes a path that compounds without requiring more of your time.

None of these need to be chosen now. The five paths share a substrate - the positioning underneath them. That's what we're locking right now. Everything else gets easier to decide once that's set.
